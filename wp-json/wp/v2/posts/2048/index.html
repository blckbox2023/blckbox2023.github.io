Over the past two years, Ukraine has seen a wave of high-profile arrests: Supreme Court Chief Justice Vsevolod Kniaziev caught with a $2.7 million bribe; the head of Khmelnytskyi\u2019s disability commission Tetiana Krupa found with piles of cash at home; MP Mykola Tyshchenko involved in a scandal over kidnapping a soldier; and a warrant issued for former Health Minister Maksym Stepanov, now hiding abroad. Yet none of these cases have reached a conclusion. None of the suspects have been punished. In fact, nearly all of them remain free.<\/em><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Since Ukraine gained independence, many citizens have believed one thing: in this country, the corrupt go unpunished. Everything is negotiable. Any case can be closed. There are very few examples of top officials actually being convicted and held accountable. But there are countless cases that simply disappeared and were quietly forgotten.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

We decided to check whether these headline-making scandals from the past few years have gone the same way. Have the key players who shocked the country with their greed and arrogance faced any consequences?<\/p>\n\n\n\n
