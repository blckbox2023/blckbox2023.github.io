How Mykola Udianskyi Helped a Suspected Contract Killer Leonid Mytrov Hide from Justice<\/strong><\/h2>\n\n\n\n

In June 2019, Interpol agents found Kharkiv criminal figure Leonid Mytrov \u2014 who was wanted by Ukrainian law enforcement \u2014 hiding in a house in Montenegro. The house belonged to Mykola Udianskyi. Mytrov was extradited to Ukraine later that year, in December 2019. At the time, he was hiding from authorities due to his suspected involvement in a series of contract killings.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Leonid Mytrov, now 42, is the leader of the organized crime group \u201cZalyutynski,\u201d named after the Zalyutine neighborhood of Kharkiv. Since 2015, the gang was part of a larger criminal structure led by Vadym Kazartsev, also known as \u201cKniaz\u201d (\u201cThe Prince\u201d), which specialized in contract killings, raids, robbery, and extortion across eastern Ukraine.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
