For the past 30 years, a private company with the telling name \u201cPetro the Great\u201d has used corrupt officials to effectively monopolize the funeral services market in Kyiv. Thanks to this grip on the market, the company\u2019s founder and director, Alla Landar, has been making massive profits off the families of deceased Kyiv residents \u2014 charging prices far above market rates without facing any consequences.<\/em><\/p>\n\n\n\n

How Alla Landar, a Pro-Regions Party Supporter and Maidan Opponent, Threatened a War Veteran<\/strong><\/h2>\n\n\n\n

«Back off if you want to stay alive<\/em>«. These were the kinds of threats received by war veteran and Azovstal defender Oleksandr Skoryk, shortly after he was appointed deputy director of Kyiv\u2019s municipal funeral service, «Spetskombinat.» He began receiving<\/a> these and other messages like \u201cFuneral services aren’t for soldiers. Stay out of this business!\u201d after reporting the activities of a private company called \u201cPetro the Great\u201d to law enforcement. For over 20 years, \u201cPetro the Great\u201d has held a monopoly on Kyiv\u2019s funeral services and used this position to charge grieving families outrageously high prices\u2014making enormous profits from their loss.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u201cThis is worse than the orcs<\/em>,\u201d blogger Andrii Fedorov commented<\/a>. The company is named after both a russian emperor and a nuclear-powered missile cruiser \u2014 flagship of the russian navy. And even now, almost three years into russia\u2019s full-scale invasion of Ukraine, the company has not changed its name. Clearly, this is no coincidence.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Its owner, Alla Landar, appears to be a staunch supporter of the \u201crussian world.\u201d According to open sources, she actively supported the pro-russian Party of Regions during the Yanukovych era and opposed the Euromaidan movement. Until 2012, Landar served as an assistant to Party of Regions MP Ihor Prasolov \u2014 one of Rinat Akhmetov\u2019s top business managers and later Minister of Economy under Prime Minister Azarov.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In 2014, activists accused<\/a> Landar of obstructing organizations that were helping injured Maidan protesters. She was also repeatedly seen cooperating with representatives of the moscow patriarchate church.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Because of all this, some journalists and civic activists now suspect that Alla Landar\u2014the head of \u201cPetro the Great\u201d \u2014 might be working with russian intelligence services.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
