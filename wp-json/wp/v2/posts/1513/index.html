Despite being inactive during martial law, members of the Central Election Commission of Ukraine continue to receive salaries twice as high as soldiers defending the country on the frontlines. Over three years of war, while ordinary Ukrainians raise funds for drones and power stations, CEC members Pavlo Liubchenko, Oleh Didenko, Oleksandra Karmaza, Serhii Pustovii, and Serhii Dubovyk have purchased apartments, cars, land, and invested in securities.<\/em><\/p>\n\n\n\n

While Ukraine\u2019s government seeks international funding for defense and recovery, three years after russia\u2019s full-scale invasion, the state still pays high salaries and bonuses to many officials. Yet, many of them seem to provide little benefit to the nation or its people during this difficult and exhausting war.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

How the Government Spends Tens of Millions of Hryvnias on Tourism Development During Wartime<\/strong><\/h2>\n\n\n\n

Let\u2019s take the State Agency for Tourism Development as an example. During wartime, with Ukraine\u2019s airspace closed and the few tourists who visit coming out of curiosity about the war, talking about tourism development seems unrealistic. One would expect such an agency to suspend operations and minimize its budget during wartime. Yet, that hasn\u2019t happened. Despite hardly having any work, agency employees continue to receive salaries, bonuses, and additional payments.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Maryana Oleskiv, head of the agency, earned 166,400 UAH as her salary for May 2024, along with a 113,900 UAH bonus. But she wasn\u2019t the only one rewarded \u2014 22 other employees, responsible for «developing tourism» in a country at war, received 1 million UAH as salaries and another 500,000 UAH as bonuses in just one month.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In the first ten months of 2024, the state spent 15.76 million UAH on salaries and bonuses for the State Agency for Tourism Development. Of that, 5.5 million UAH went solely to bonuses. What were these bonuses for? Perhaps for posting greetings<\/a> on social media for World Cleanup Day? Or for news<\/a> on their official website about the start of the beach season and a list of recreational lakes? In reality, this government agency does almost nothing useful today, yet nothing has been done to fix this. As a result, millions of hryvnias are spent every month to maintain staff who provide no real benefit to a nation at war.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Meanwhile, these individuals are acquiring new properties and cars. For instance, Ihor Chava, Head of the Regional Tourism Infrastructure Coordination Division at the Department of Tourism Development, purchased a 67.5-square-meter apartment in Drohobych, Lviv region, in 2023. According to YouControl<\/a>, the market value of this apartment ranges from 3 to 3.5 million UAH.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
